विभाजन
The event that shaped South Asia forever
The partition of British India in 1947 was one of the most significant events in South Asian history. Punjab, the "Land of Five Rivers," was divided between Pakistan and India, reshaping millions of lives forever. It remains one of the largest mass migrations in human history, affecting over 14 million people.
August 14, 1947: Pakistan gained independence under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the Father of the Nation
The Radcliffe Line divided Punjab, separating communities, families, and friendships that had coexisted for centuries
Over 7 million Muslims migrated to West Punjab (Pakistan), while Hindus and Sikhs moved east in the largest mass migration ever
Lahore, the cultural and intellectual capital of Punjab, became part of Pakistan and remains the heart of Pakistani Punjab
Despite the immense tragedy and loss, Pakistan emerged as a sovereign nation with rich cultural heritage, resilience, and hope
The stories of partition are preserved through oral histories, literature, and the Bronze Migration Portal on this app

प्राचीन इतिहास
5,000+ years of civilization
The land that is now Pakistan has been home to some of the world's oldest and most sophisticated civilizations, spanning over 5,000 years of recorded history. From the advanced urban planning of Mohenjo-daro to the Greco-Buddhist art of Gandhara, this region has been at the crossroads of major world civilizations.
Indus Valley Civilization (3300-1300 BC): Mohenjo-daro and Harappa were among the world's most advanced ancient cities with grid streets, drainage, and public baths
Gandhara Civilization: Taxila University (Takshashila) was one of the world's earliest universities, predating Oxford by over 1,500 years
Persian & Greek Influence: Alexander the Great passed through in 326 BC, leaving a legacy of Greco-Buddhist art and culture
Mughal Empire (1526-1857): Lahore became a jewel of Mughal architecture with the Badshahi Mosque, Lahore Fort, and Shalimar Gardens
Sikh Empire (1799-1849): Maharaja Ranjit Singh ruled Punjab from Lahore, expanding the Lahore Fort and preserving Mughal heritage
British Colonial Period (1849-1947): Railways, canals, and institutions were built, along with the seeds of the independence movement

आधुनिक इतिहास
From independence to a nuclear power
Since independence in 1947, Pakistan has grown into a dynamic nation with significant achievements in defense, science, infrastructure, culture, and technology. The journey from a newly born nation to the world's 7th nuclear power is a story of resilience and ambition.
1956: Pakistan became an Islamic Republic with its first constitution, establishing democratic governance
1960s: 'Decade of Development' - rapid industrialization, the new capital Islamabad was built, and Pakistan's GDP grew at 6%+ annually
1998: Pakistan becomes the 7th nuclear power in the world, ensuring strategic defense capability
CPEC (2015+): China-Pakistan Economic Corridor - $62 billion infrastructure investment transforming roads, ports, and energy
IT & Tech: Rapidly growing freelance and tech industry - one of the top 4 freelancing countries globally with $2.6B+ IT exports
2023+: Digital Pakistan initiative expanding 5G, fintech, e-commerce, and startup ecosystem

राजधानी
One of the most beautiful capitals in the world
Islamabad, the capital city, is one of the most beautiful and well-planned capitals in the world. Nestled at the foot of the Margalla Hills, it combines modern governance with natural beauty. Built as a purpose-designed capital in the 1960s, it features wide tree-lined avenues, modern architecture, and abundant green spaces.
Founded in 1960 as a purpose-built capital to replace Karachi, designed by Greek architect Constantinos Doxiadis
Home to Faisal Mosque, the largest mosque in South Asia with capacity for 300,000 worshippers
Parliament House, Supreme Court, Aiwan-e-Sadr (Presidential Palace), and all federal institutions
Margalla Hills National Park offers hiking trails, wildlife, monkeys, leopards, and stunning panoramic views
F-7 Jinnah Super, F-6 Super Market, and vibrant food scenes from street food to five-star dining
Diplomatic enclave hosts 75+ embassies, international organizations, and United Nations offices
